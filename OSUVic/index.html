OSUVic is a new climate model developed at Oregon State University by Tiago Silva and Andreas Schmittner. It consists of the UVic Earth System Model, which we amended with a simplified atmospheric general circulation model (Planet Simulator). A more detailed description can be found here. The movie below shows two years of daily data of the vertically integrated water vapor content in the atmosphere and orography (0 m from UVic grid, and 500 m and 1 km isoline difference between 1 km and 6 km from PlaSim grid) from the T42 model version. This is a contribution of the NSF funded project PALEOVAR.
