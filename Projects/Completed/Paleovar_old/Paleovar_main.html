 Little is known about how climate variability will respond to global warming or how regional climate will change. Paleoclimatic data provide information on climate variability beyond the range of modern human experience.

Project PALEOVAR is a coordinated approach to understand the interaction between short-term variability and the climatic mean state over the past 50,000 years.

Data sources include ice cores and speleothem (cave formation) records from continental settings and marine sedimentary records.

Data analysis techniques include the adaptation of state-of-the-art statistical methods to the study of paleoclimate time series. Integration of paleoclimate data with a hierarchy of models include a global Earth System Model of Intermediate Complexity and regional and global scale atmospheric general circulation models.
PALEOVAR is funded by the Paleoclimate Program of the US National Science Foundation through 2011.
